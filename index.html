Premier League

Bournemouth

"The Pacific Cherries" — Oceania & Pacific Island project

Turn Vitality Stadium into the home of Pacific football. Every signing comes from Australia, New Zealand, Japan, South Korea, or the Pacific Islands. Nobody else in the game is touching this market — you are buying cheap and building something no one has ever seen in a Premier League career mode.

Formation philosophy

4-3-3 built on pace and athleticism. Pacific Island players bring physical power, Australians bring grit, and East Asian players bring technical precision. The combination produces a team that is relentlessly energetic and hard to play against at home.

Premier League

Everton

"The Home Nations" — Purely British. No exceptions.

Every player English, Scottish, Welsh, or Northern Irish. No imports. No loopholes. A new stadium, a new era, and a squad built entirely from British talent. In an era when every top club floods their squad with foreign stars, Everton become the one club that bets everything on home-grown quality — and proves the point.

Formation philosophy

4-3-3 or 4-4-2 classic. British football at its best is direct, physical, and relentless — but it is also technically gifted when the players are chosen right. You are not playing kick-and-rush. You are proving that the best players from these four nations, in the right system, can compete with anyone. The formation should feel unmistakably British without being primitive.

Storyline tags
England, Scotland, Wales, N. IrelandNew stadium identityZero importsHome-grown onlyStatement save
Why it works for 5 seasons

British football produces more Premier League-calibre players than most people realise — they just get overlooked at the big six. You scour the Football League and lower leagues for gems that no one else is taking seriously. Season 1-2 you establish the project. Season 3 you are competitive. Season 4 you are top half. Season 5 you are making the argument loud and clear from a brand new stadium on Merseyside that British football never needed to be rescued.

Crystal Palace

"Les Aigles" — The French Eagles of South London

Palace are the Eagles. In French that is Les Aigles. Build the entire squad from France — the nation producing more elite talent than anywhere else right now. Ligue 1 bargains, second-string players at PSG and Monaco, academy graduates from Lyon and Rennes. The pipeline never runs dry.

4-3-1-2 or 4-2-3-1 centered around a genuine French #10. France produces extraordinary creative midfielders and the system is built around one. Technical, possession-based, with pace wide and a striker who holds the line. Mirrors how the French national team plays when everything clicks.

West Ham United

"The Hammers" — European giants. Literal hammers.

West Ham get relegated and you rebuild using one rule: every outfield player must be physically imposing. European players, minimum 6ft 1in, built like they were cut from iron. Scandinavian towers, Balkan warriors, Dutch and German powerhouses. The London Stadium becomes a ground that opponents genuinely dread visiting. Not a big team — a massive one.

Formation philosophy

3-5-2 or 5-3-2 built entirely around dominating aerial duels, second balls, and set pieces. Every corner and free kick is a genuine goal threat. You defend with your size, transition with your legs, and score through power and physicality. In the Championship you are too big for every opponent. In the Prem you become a set-piece machine that wins points others cannot. Technically gifted European players who also happen to be built like centre-backs at every position.

Storyline tags

Why it works for 5 seasons

Season 1 Championship promotion — you physically bully every team in the division. Season 2 PL survival through set pieces and organised defending. Season 3 your European giants start showing their technical quality too. Season 4 Europa League. Season 5 you have built the most physically imposing squad in the Premier League and teams gameplan specifically against your size. Scandinavia, the Netherlands, Germany, and the Balkans have enough 6ft+ players with faces to run this for a decade.
